The purpose of this study is to modify materials for a new, experimental fear reduction intervention.

You may be presented with experimental fear reduction interventions. Alternatively, you may be asked to complete “control” tasks, which are tasks that are similar to experimental fear reduction techniques, but not designed to reduce fear. You may be asked to complete questionnaires in which you describe your thoughts and mood, a task that requires you to look at words and determine where targets appear, and a task in which you read brief stories, answer questions, and complete word fragments. For some participants, being asked questions about their thoughts and mood can be temporarily distressing. At the end of the study, you will receive more detail about the study's rationale and design.

You are part of a “pilot” group, which is designed to help us edit our materials and procedures. You will not be asked to complete the full experiment. Rather, you will be asked to do some of the tasks listed above and we will ask your opinions about some of the procedures via questionnaires and interviews.

Remember that participation in this study is voluntary, and you may end your individual session participation at any time. Please ask the researcher, or contact studyteam@mindtrails.org if you have any questions about the study, or wish to withdraw from the study. You will not be penalized for withdrawing from the study.

MindTrails does not provide specific diagnostic or medical advice on our website. The study you are about to complete is an experimental anxiety intervention study. All data collected through the Project Implicit Mental Health website are confidential and are only accessible by the research team.
