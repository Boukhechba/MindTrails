Online Future Thinking Training Program Information

We are currently investigating experimental approaches to encourage healthy future thinking. The experimental approaches used in the study are based on research showing that anxious people tend to interpret ambiguous situations as threatening. It has been proposed that the tendency to interpret things in a threatening way plays a role in the onset of, and recovery from, anxiety problems. We think a similar process may occur when people worry a lot about how events will turn out in the future.

In the current study, we are evaluating if it is possible to change future thinking patterns over the Internet, and whether changing these thinking patterns will increase optimism and improve mood. You were asked to read and imagine yourself in a series of brief stories. In the two main active conditions, the last word (or word fragment) gave all brief stories a positive meaning. In two alternative conditions, which we still expect to be somewhat helpful, the last word (or word fragment) gave half of the stories a positive meaning and half of the stories a negative meaning. In the “control” condition, which was not expected to be as helpful at encouraging healthy future thinking, you read stories that were not expected to raise emotional responses about the future.

If you are interested in learning more about modifying thinking patterns, see the following websites: http://www.apa.org/monitor/2011/11/behavior-change.aspx http://www.economist.com/node/18276234
