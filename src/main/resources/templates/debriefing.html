Debriefing

We are currently investigating experimental approaches to reduce anxiety. The experimental approaches used in the study are based on models of anxiety, which have shown that anxious people tend to interpret ambiguous situations as threatening. It has been proposed that the tendency to interpret things in a threatening way plays a role in the onset of, and recovery from, anxiety problems.

In the current study, we are evaluating if it is possible to administer techniques to change interpretation patterns over the Internet, and whether these programs reduce anxiety. You either participated in an imagery condition that is expected to reduce your tendency to interpret information in a negative way, or you participated in an imagery condition that may be less effective in changing interpretations. In addition, you either participated in a training condition that is expected to reduce your tendency to interpret information in a negative way, a training condition that encouraged positive and negative interpretations equally, or you did not participate in a training condition.

If you were in an imagery condition designed to change the way you interpret information, you were asked to imagine yourself in situations that make you feel anxious. If you were in an imagery condition not designed to change the way you interpret information, you were asked to imagine yourself in a situations that were fairly mundane. If you were in a training condition designed to change the way you interpret information, you were asked to read and imagine yourself in a series of brief stories. In the main active condition, the last word (or word fragment) gave all brief stories a positive meaning. In one alternative condition, which we still expect to be somewhat helpful, the last word (or word fragment) gave half of the stories a positive meaning and half of the stories a negative meaning. In the other alternative condition, you did not read any stories. We expect that imagining yourself in situations that make you feel anxious will increase positive interpretations more effectively than imagining yourself in mundane situations. In addition, we expect that the active condition will increase positive interpretations more effectively than not reading stories. Additionally, we predict that imaging yourself in situations that make you feel anxious and active training will decrease anxiety symptoms, though this is a new training program so part of the current study is to determine if this program can work well on the Internet.
