Step 1 - Use your imagination

Please think of an upcoming situation that you expect will make you feel anxious, worried, or scared. For instance, the situation could involve public speaking (e.g., in class or at work), talking to someone in a position of authority (e.g., a boss or teacher), meeting new people, extreme worries about a situation tied to your health, work, relationships, or finances, or fears about entering a situation or encountering an object that frightens you (e.g., seeing a spider or snake, flying in an airplane, or being up high). Pick a situation that you worry might go badly, or that you expect will evoke symptoms related to anxiety (e.g., racing heart, sweating). Any situation that will make you feel anxious or scared will work. If no specific situation is upcoming, then just select a situation that you expect would make you feel anxious or scared IF you were to be in that situation.

Step 2 - Note the situation




Please generate an image of the situation that is as vivid as possible (i.e., that feels very real). Try to imagine you are actually in the situation. Play out the specific parts of the situation in your head.

Step 3 - Things you would see

Please think of at least three things you would see in the situation.

Step 4 - Things you would hear

Please think of at least three things you would hear in the situation.

Step 5 - Things you would think or feel




Please play this upcoming situation in your head for the next 20 seconds, remembering all of the things you see, hear, think, and feel. We will tell you when the 20 seconds are up.
